John Franklin Crenshaw

I am a student studying Physics and German at Duke University, with a primary focus on neutrinos, astrophysics, and cosmology. I work with Prof. Kate Scholberg in the Duke Neutrino and Cosmology Group on the HALO supernova neutrino detector. My research focuses on data unfolding methods and understanding how astrophysical parameters influence the analysis of HALO data.

I also work with Dr. Andreas Haungs and Agnieszka Leszczynska at the Institute for Nuclear Physics in Karlsruhe, Germany. Using the IceTop air shower array, we analyze the muon component of cosmic ray air showers for use in cosmic ray mass composition studies.

I am a Duke Faculty Scholar.
