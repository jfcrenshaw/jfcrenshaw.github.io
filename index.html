John Franklin Crenshaw

I am a student studying Physics and German at Duke University, with a primary focus on neutrinos, astrophysics, and cosmology. I work with Dr. Kate Scholberg in the Duke Neutrino and Cosmology Group on the HALO supernova neutrino detector. My research focuses on data unfolding methods and understanding how astrophysical parameters affect the analysis of HALO data.

I also work with Dr. Andreas Haungs and Agnieszka Leszczynska at the Karlsruhe Institute of Technology in Karlsruhe, Germany. Using IceTop, the cosmic ray detector at the South Pole, we analyze the muon component of cosmic ray airshowers for use in cosmic ray mass composition studies.

I am a Duke Faculty Scholar.
