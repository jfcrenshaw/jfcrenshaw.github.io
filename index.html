John Franklin Crenshaw

I am a student studying Physics and German at Duke University, with a primary focus on neutrinos, astrophysics, and cosmology. I work with Dr. Kate Scholberg in the Duke Neutrino and Cosmology Group. My research focuses on data unfolding methods for the Helium and Lead Observatory (HALO), a supernova neutrino detector based at SNOLAB in Ontario, Canada. I also use Bayesian statistics to study how knowledge of supernova distance and supernova neutrino flux constraints affects the analysis of HALO data.

I also work with Dr. Andreas Haungs and Agnieszka Leszczynska at the Karlsruhe Institute of Technology to analyze the cosmic air shower signals in IceTop at the south pole. The goal of our work is to quantify the muon component of the signal for use in cosmic ray mass composition studies.

I am a Duke Faculty Scholar.
