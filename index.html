John Franklin Crenshaw

I am a physics PhD student at the University of Washington working with Prof. Andy Connolly on machine learning in astrophysics and cosmology. I am a member the DIRAC Institute in UW's Astronomy Department and the Dark Energy Science Collaboration (DESC) of the Vera C. Rubin Observatory. My current focus is on photo-z estimation and the deconvolution of spectra from broadband photometry, using tools such as normalizing flows, neural ODE's, Gaussian processes, and more.

Prior to UW, I graduated summa cum laude with highest distinction from Duke University. I was a Duke Faculty Scholar working with
Prof. Kate Scholberg in the Duke Neutrino and Cosmology Group. Using detector simulations and Bayesian analysis, I improved data unfolding methods for the HALO supernova neutrino detector.
