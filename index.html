John Franklin Crenshaw

In the fall of 2019 I will begin my Physics PhD at the University of Washington in Seattle, where I will work with Prof. Andy Connolly on survey cosmology with the Large Synoptic Survey Telescope (LSST).

I graduated summa cum laude with a B.S. in physics from Duke University. I was a Duke Faculty Scholar working with
Prof. Kate Scholberg in the Duke Neutrino and Cosmology Group. My research focused on improving data unfolding methods for the HALO supernova neutrino detector.

I also worked with Dr. Andreas Haungs and Agnieszka Leszczynska at the Institute for Nuclear Physics in Karlsruhe, Germany. Using the IceTop air shower array, we analyzed the muon fraction of cosmic ray air showers.
